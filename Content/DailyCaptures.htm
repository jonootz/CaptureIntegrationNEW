“Part-Time Work Ethic: Should Teens go for it?,” Chapter 1 Summary

In Dennis McLellan’s article, “Part-Time Work Ethic: Should Teens go for it?,” he discusses the pros and cons of teenagers having part-time jobs while going to school. First, there are many positive aspects to teens working. McLellan states that having a job “Builds character, teaches responsibility, and prepares them for the adult world,” by showing teens how to complete projects thoroughly and on time (280). Clearly, jobs can be beneficial to teenagers. Along with all of these positive aspects; however, there are also negative effects. In fact, teenage workers have been “stealing money,” “merchandise,” and even “showing up to work under the influence of drugs or alcohol” (281-82). The author makes a point that not everything about part-time jobs is beneficial to students. As a teenager I worked part-time at a clothing retailer. I definitely learned personal responsibility in relation to both my work schedule and financial responsibility. I would most certainly fall on the side of the positive aspect of teens working.

Class Topic: How to Outline Notes

  1. The first level is reserved for each new topic/idea and is very general.
    1. This concept must always apply to the level above it (I)
      1. This concept must always apply to the level above it (a)
      2. This is a second supporting piece of information for the level above it (a) but is equal to the previous information (i)
      3. This information is a sister to (i) and (ii)
    2. This concept applies to the level above it (I) and is a “sister” to (a)
  2. You don’t have to use Roman Numerals, Letter, and Numbers – try only indents, dashes, and bullets.
  3. Outlining requires listening and writing in points in an organizational pattern based on space and indentation
    1. Advantages to outlining
      1. It is well - organized
      2. It records relationships and content
      3. It reduces editing and is easy to review by turning the main points into questions
    2. Disadvantages to outlining
      1. It requires more thought during class for accurate organization
      2. It does not always show relationships by sequence
      3. It doesn’t work well if the lecture is moving at a quick pace
